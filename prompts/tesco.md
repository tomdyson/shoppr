# Tesco Store Layout

Tesco stores typically have produce near the entrance, moving clockwise through the store.

Organize items into these areas, ordered by typical Tesco layout:

1. **produce** (area_order: 1) - Fresh fruits, vegetables, prepared salads
2. **bakery** (area_order: 2) - In-store bakery, bread aisle
3. **dairy** (area_order: 3) - Milk, cheese, yogurt, butter, eggs, cream
4. **meat** (area_order: 4) - Fresh meat counter, packaged meat, fish
5. **deli** (area_order: 5) - Deli counter, olives, cooked meats
6. **world_foods** (area_order: 6) - International foods section
7. **pantry** (area_order: 7) - Tins, jars, pasta, rice, cooking sauces
8. **breakfast** (area_order: 8) - Cereals, porridge, spreads
9. **snacks** (area_order: 9) - Crisps, nuts, biscuits
10. **confectionery** (area_order: 10) - Chocolate, sweets
11. **drinks** (area_order: 11) - Soft drinks, juice, water
12. **tea_coffee** (area_order: 12) - Tea, coffee, hot chocolate
13. **alcohol** (area_order: 13) - Beer, wine, spirits
14. **frozen** (area_order: 14) - Frozen foods (often at back of store)
15. **household** (area_order: 15) - Cleaning, laundry, paper products
16. **health_beauty** (area_order: 16) - Toiletries, medicines, beauty
17. **baby** (area_order: 17) - Baby food, nappies, baby care
18. **pet** (area_order: 18) - Pet food, pet supplies
19. **other** (area_order: 19) - Miscellaneous

Note: This is for larger Tesco stores. Express stores have a more compact layout.
